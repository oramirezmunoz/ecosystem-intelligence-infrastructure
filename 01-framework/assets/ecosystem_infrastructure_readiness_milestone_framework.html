Ecosystem infrastructure readiness — milestone framework

Eight deliverables, one gate:
no ecosystem infrastructure budget moves until all exist

Complete scope before rules. Rules before data. Data before evidence.
The sequence is not a suggestion.

Step 1

Scope

Use-case brief

Define what the infrastructure is actually for. Who participates, what they exchange, and why they'd show up. Without this, every downstream decision has no anchor.

Scope phase
Step 2

Scope

Stakeholder map

Name every party who sends or receives value in the ecosystem. Corporates, startups, SMEs, intermediaries, public institutions, research organisations — map all sides before assuming you know who it is.

Scope phase
Step 3

Rules

Rulebook v0.1

First draft of what is allowed, what triggers a match, and what doesn't. Not final — but written. An ecosystem without written rules is a network pretending to be one.

Rules phase
Step 4

Rules

Sharing rules

Who sees what, and when. Defines the trust architecture. Any actor sharing intent signals needs to know the rules before they'll share anything real.

Rules phase
Step 5

Data

Data inventory

Catalogue what signals already exist, where they live, and how structured they are. Passive interest, active collaborations, procurement history, funding signals — inventory first, ingest second.

Data phase
Step 6

Data

Trust checklist

Before any data moves, verify consent, provenance, and access rights. The checklist is the paper trail that makes the infrastructure legally and operationally defensible.

Data phase
Step 7

Evidence

Exchange test

Run one real exchange end-to-end. Not a demo. Not a simulation. One actor, one counterpart, one outcome — observed and documented. The moment the infrastructure stops being a plan.

Evidence phase
Step 8

Evidence

Scale readiness

Assess what breaks if participation triples. Infrastructure, governance, support. If the answer is "we'll figure it out," the ecosystem is not ready. If it's written down, it might be.
