# Ecosystem Intelligence Infrastructure (EII)

An open framework for building the information infrastructure 
that makes innovation ecosystems intelligent about themselves.

---

## The Problem

Innovation and entrepreneurship ecosystems generate enormous amounts 
of valuable information — about startups, investors, research, talent, 
corporate needs, policy instruments, and market signals.

Most of it never reaches the people who need it.

It sits fragmented across organisations, databases, reports, and 
conversations. No one has the mandate to consolidate it. No one 
translates it into action. The result:

- Ecosystem operators make coordination decisions based on partial data
- Startups can't find early adopters who are actively looking for them
- Programs and instruments are built that don't match real needs
- Resources are wasted at scale, continuously

This is not a technology problem. It is an infrastructure problem.

---

## The Thesis

Every functioning ecosystem needs a permanent, dedicated layer of 
infrastructure whose job is to aggregate, interpret, and route 
information to the actors who need it — just as cities need roads, 
energy grids, and water systems to function.

We call this the **Ecosystem Intelligence Infrastructure (EII)**.

EII is not a platform. It is not a project. It is a permanent public 
good — a designed system that makes an ecosystem continuously 
intelligent about itself.

---

## The Framework

EII is built on six layers:

| Layer | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **0. Sharing** | The prerequisite. Actors share signals only when trust, incentives, and governance make it safe and worthwhile |
| **1. Collection** | Systematic gathering of signals from all actor types |
| **2. Aggregation** | Turning raw signals into a coherent picture of the ecosystem |
| **3. Translation** | Packaging insights in ways that are meaningful and actionable for each actor type |
| **4. Distribution** | Getting the right information to the right actors at the right time |
| **5. Governance** | Who owns and operates this, with what mandate and funding logic |
